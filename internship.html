03

Summer Internship — Report & Viva

The work stint is behind us — what remains is the report and the viva voce, and that's where the marks are. Below is the full AICTE (SEC-702) engagement process, the exact report structure, and how the evaluation works.

SEC 702 · Skill Enhancement Course 4 credits 8 weeks · ~20 hrs/week Report → review → viva voce

The six-step process

Steps 1–4 are done. You are here → steps 5 & 6.

  1. Orientation session. ✅ Done — the overview of what to expect and how the internship bridges academic learning and real-world practice.
  2. Identify an internship. ✅ Done — an opportunity aligned with your career goals and academic background, found via job boards, networking or professional associations.
  3. Internship agreement form. ✅ Done — completed jointly by you, your mentor and the internship coordinator in the industry. Keep your copy; it goes in the report.
  4. The internship itself. ✅ Done — eight weeks at ~20 hours per week on a schedule arranged by your mentor, with regular check-ins and progress reviews.
  5. Submit the report. ⏳ Now — assemble the report in the exact structure listed on the right and submit it to the department. The examiner's review happens about two weeks after submission, so submit early enough to leave room before the viva.
  6. Viva voce evaluation. ⏳ Next — an oral exam where you present your experience, learning and contributions. The internal examiner evaluates your submission.

What the viva actually tests

  • Summarise your role, key projects and the skills you applied
  • Connect academic theory to what you actually did on the job
  • Explain challenges faced — and specifically how you addressed them
  • Reflect on your professional development and career direction
  • Bring in supervisor feedback and industry insights you picked up
  • Answer probing follow-ups — examiners dig into your understanding
The viva is graded on your ability to articulate growth and readiness for professional work — not on how glamorous the company was. A tight story (role → work → problem → solution → learning) beats a long list of tasks.

Report structure

The prescribed contents, in order. Nothing extra, nothing missing.

  1. FrontFront page — student name, course, internship company, duration, mentor
  2. DocInternship agreement form — the signed copy
  3. DocInternship certificate — on company letterhead
  4. Ch 1Introduction & background of the company — sector, scale, products/services, where your team fit
  5. Ch 2Roles & responsibilities as an intern — your actual mandate, tools used, who you reported to
  6. Ch 3Weekly work allotment & completion report — week-by-week: what was assigned, what was delivered (this is why the diary mattered)
  7. Ch 4Challenges & solutions — concrete problems you hit and exactly how you resolved or worked around them
  8. Ch 5Learning from the internship — skills gained, theory applied in practice, workplace culture and dynamics understood
  9. EndConclusion — how the internship shaped your career goals

Before you submit — checklist

  • Certificate and agreement form scanned/attached in the right order
  • Company facts verified (names, dates, designations spelled right)
  • Weekly table complete — no missing weeks
  • Each challenge paired with a solution, not just complaints
  • Learning section maps to your track (Fin/Mkt/HR) and its papers
  • Proofread; consistent formatting; mentor has seen the final draft
Course outcomes on record: application of theoretical knowledge to real-world scenarios · development of professional skills and networking · understanding workplace culture and dynamics · hands-on experience in your chosen field. Make sure your report visibly demonstrates all four.
