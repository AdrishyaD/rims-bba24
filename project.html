04

Semester Project

The independent research project runs across Semesters V–VI: proposal and defence this semester, full report and final viva before the Semester VI exams. Everything below — timeline, title rules, report skeleton and formatting — follows the AICTE major-project guidelines.

Proposal = Chapters 1–3 Proposal due ≥2 weeks before Sem V exams Final report ≥2 weeks before Sem VI exams Defence + viva voce, marks compiled together

Timeline & process

From topic to final viva — the official sequence.

  1. Pick a topic, get a supervisor. You work under a project supervisor appointed by the institute. The proposal is submitted right after the research-methods unit — i.e. this semester.
  2. Write the proposal (Chapters 1–3). Introduction, literature review and research methodology. Your supervisor must approve the title before you proceed, and the research committee ratifies all topics.
  3. Submit two copies. At least two copies of the proposal go to the Project Coordinator no later than two weeks before the Semester V final examination.
  4. Defend the proposal. You present before panels formed by the project coordinator on a set day and are awarded marks. You'll receive corrections — work them in and get supervisor sign-off to continue.
  5. Complete Chapters 4 & 5. Collect and analyse data, then write the findings and the summary/conclusions/recommendations chapters.
  6. Final submission & viva voce. Once the supervisor approves the finished project, it goes to the project coordinator, who arranges the final defence and VIVA VOCE. Those marks are added to your proposal-defence marks. Submit two bound copies to the institute at least two weeks before the Semester VI final exam. (If unsuccessful, resubmission rules from the academic policy apply.)

Choosing a title — the rules

  • Clear and specific to a real problem
  • New and original — not a replica of anyone else's work
  • Similar topics between students are avoided
  • Supervisor approval at proposal level is mandatory
  • The research committee must ratify every topic

Formatting guidelines

FontTimes New Roman · 12 pt body · 14 pt for topics & titles
Spacing1.5 lines throughout
HeadingsTopics and subtopics bolded, never underlined
Margins1″ top, bottom & right · 1.25″ left (for binding)
TablesLarge tables may drop to 10–11 pt to keep margins; every table/figure needs a number, heading and a finding-and-analysis line ("Table 4.1 shows that…", never "the table below")
NumbersSentences must not begin with a numeral or percentage
PaginationBottom of page, centred; main document starts at page 1 in Chapter One (front matter in Roman numerals)
ReferencesAPA style, throughout
BindingHard copy, blue, preferably hardcover book binding
Evaluation is based on the completed project, the proposal presentation, and the viva voce. Plagiarism is the fastest way to sink it — the critical-review section exists precisely to prove your gap is real and your work original.

Report skeleton

Every element, in order — front matter through appendices.

  1. iTitle page — topic in upper case, centred, 14 pt; your name, class & year; supervisor's name & designation; "Research Project Submitted in Partial Fulfilment for the Degree of Bachelor in Business Administration"; month & year
  2. iiDeclaration — signed by you ("This project is my original work and has not been presented for a degree in any other university or for any other award") and by the supervisor confirming the work was done under their supervision
  3. iiiDedication — optional, ≤25 words, own page
  4. ivAcknowledgement — ~150 words; credit for guidance and help during the study
  5. vAbstract — ≤300 words, one single-spaced paragraph on one page: purpose & objectives · significance · methodology · findings · recommendations
  6. vi+Table of contents · List of tables · List of figures — all tables/figures numbered, headed, APA style
  7. Ch 1Introduction
    • 1.1 Introduction — overview of chapter contents
    • 1.2 Background of the study (specifics, citations, build the case)
    • 1.3 Problem statement — a paragraph that shows clearly what the problem is and what the research solves, with authoritative sources on what's been done and what's missing
    • 1.4 Objectives — general goal + specific objectives that are SMART and within scope
    • 1.5 Research questions — same count as objectives; never yes/no framing
    • 1.6 Significance — who benefits and how
    • 1.7 Scope — concept, content, geographical and time scope
    • 1.8 Organisation of the study, chapter by chapter
  8. Ch 2Literature review
    • 2.1 Introduction · 2.2 Theoretical literature — concepts, definitions and theories your study uses
    • 2.3 Similar projects — discuss at least five similar works (global and local), APA-cited
    • 2.4 Critical review & research-gap identification — the unique innovation of your project; without a clear gap it reads as plagiarised
    • 2.5 Summary of the critical issues raised
  9. Ch 3Research methodology — research design; population and sample (random / stratified / purposive sampling); data types and tools of collection (questionnaire, schedule, interview, observation); data organisation and analysis plan (tabulation, SPSS/Excel, central tendency, correlation, chi-square, t-test as applicable)
  10. Ch 4Data analysis, presentation & interpretation — findings organised objective by objective; each table/figure followed by its finding and analysis; check every objective is addressed
  11. Ch 5Summary, conclusions & recommendations
    • 5.1 Introduction · 5.2 Summary of findings by objective
    • 5.3 Conclusions — direct answers to the project objectives
    • 5.4 Recommendations — in line with the study area and conclusions
    • 5.5 Suggestions for further study
  12. 6.0References — APA style, everything cited
  13. 7.0Appendices — A: questionnaire · B: response summary sheet · C: other evidence such as pictures
Tie the topic to your specialisation — a bank/NBFC or markets study for Finance, a brand, service or consumer study for Marketing, a people/teams/talent study for HR — and, wherever you can, grow it out of the business problem you saw at your internship. You already have the access, the context and half the data.
