CommonCC501Strategic Management3L:1T:0P · 4 cr

How firms build and sustain competitive advantage — thinking like a general manager who must pull accounting, finance, HR, information systems, marketing and operations into one cohesive whole.

Objectives

  • Enhance the ability to do the job of a general manager responsible for strategic performance — integrating the different functional areas of business into a cohesive whole
  • Analyse the competitive situation and evaluate the challenges managers face in implementing and evaluating strategies across businesses, industries and cultures

Units

  1. 1 · Introduction to Strategy

    Strategy — concept, relevance, role and benefits; importance of strategic management; the strategic management process; levels of strategy; approaches to strategic decision-making; strategic intent — vision, mission, goals and objectives; strategy and corporate governance; social responsibility and ethics in strategic management. Also traces how strategic-management thinking has evolved over the decades.

  2. 2 · Strategic Analysis

    Environmental appraisal — scanning the technological, social, cultural, demographic, political and legal environment; components and analysis of the general (external) environment; industry analysis — analysis of the competitive environment; analysis of the internal environment — strategic capability, nature, characteristics, types and approaches; value chain analysis; experience curve; SWOT analysis; BCG Matrix; GE nine-cell Matrix. Goal: judge the value-capturing ability of incumbents and forecast industry profitability from shifts in underlying forces.

  3. 3 · Strategy Formulation

    Business strategy — generic strategies; functional areas and the link between business strategy and functional strategy. Corporate strategy — creating value and diversification; strategic alliances; international expansion strategies (multi-domestic approach, franchising, licensing, joint ventures); strategies of growth, stability and renewal — concentrated growth, product development, integration, diversification; renewal via retrenchment and turnaround; strategic fundamentals of mergers & acquisitions; Blue Ocean strategy.

  4. 4 · Strategy Implementation & Control

    Structural, functional/operational and behavioural implementation; strategy evaluation and control; strategic leadership; strategic control and corporate governance; issues in strategy implementation; creating effective organizational designs; strategy and society; managing innovation and fostering corporate entrepreneurship; integrating functional plans and policies; operational control and an overview of management control — turning internal alignment and temporary opportunities into sustainable advantage.

Core texts

Wheelen, Hunger, Hoffman, Bamford & Kansal — Concepts in Strategic Management & Business Policy (15e, Pearson) · Rothaermel — Strategic Management (5e Indian, McGraw Hill). Key readings: Porter — The Five Competitive Forces that Shape Strategy (HBR) · Collis & Montgomery — Competing on Resources · Kim & Mauborgne — Blue Ocean Strategy · Hambrick & Fredrickson — Are You Sure You Have a Strategy? · Porter & Kramer — Competitive Advantage & CSR.

CommonCC502Logistics & Supply Chain Management3L:1T:0P · 4 cr

Managing and optimizing supply chains in modern business — logistics, strategic fit, network design, sourcing, pricing and demand management, with a running emphasis on how digital transformation and e-commerce reshape supply-chain strategy.

Objectives

  • Understand the strategic role of supply chain management in enhancing organizational competitiveness
  • Explore the design and optimization of supply chain networks, particularly for e-commerce
  • Examine the impact of sourcing and pricing decisions on supply chain efficiency and effectiveness
  • Develop skills in coordinating and managing supply and demand in a globalized market

Units

  1. 1 · Introduction to Supply Chain Management

    The role of supply chain management in organizations; achieving strategic fit; supply chain drivers and metrics — how supply chains align business strategy with operational capability to lift performance and competitiveness.

  2. 2 · Supply Chain Network Design

    Designing distribution networks with a focus on e-business applications and global supply chain networks; strategies and challenges of building supply chains that are both efficient and responsive, aligned with business objectives and dynamic global markets.

  3. 3 · Sourcing & Pricing Strategies

    Sourcing decisions and pricing management within supply chains; sourcing strategies and pricing models for revenue management and cost-efficiency, with particular focus on e-commerce dynamics.

  4. 4 · Demand & Supply Coordination

    Synchronizing demand forecasting, aggregate planning, and sales & operations planning; demand-forecasting techniques, aggregate-planning strategies, and coordination across the chain to balance demand and supply efficiently.

Core texts

Chopra & Meindl — Supply Chain Management: Strategy, Planning & Operation (7e, Pearson) · Martin Christopher — Logistics & Supply Chain Management (5e, Pearson).

Suggested cases

  • Dell's direct model in PC manufacturing — integrating supply chain and e-business
  • Starbucks' sustainable sourcing practices — a supply chain perspective
  • Walmart's supply chain management strategies for global dominance

Outcomes

  • Design and manage effective supply chains that enhance competitiveness
  • Gain practical insight into strategic sourcing, demand planning and coordination
  • Understand how digital transformation affects supply chain strategy and operations

FinanceDSE-Fin09Banking & Insurance3L:1T:0P · 4 cr

A thorough examination of the principles, practices and regulatory structures governing India's banking and insurance sectors — functions, procedures, products and services — preparing you for careers in banking, insurance and financial services.

Objectives

  • Develop understanding of the fundamental principles and functioning of banking in India
  • Explain concepts and general principles of insurance in relation to risk and its management
  • Expand knowledge of products, services and the latest technologies used in both sectors
  • Equip yourself with techniques to analyse the financial performance of banks for risk management

Units

  1. 1 · Introduction to Banking & Insurance

    Banking: evolution, structure and development of banking in India — commercial (public, private, foreign), rural and co-operative banks; role of banks in economic development; RBI — objectives, functions and developmental role; fee-based and fund-based products; types of accounts, deposits, advances and collaterals; management of bank assets & liabilities; reading a commercial bank's balance sheet and income statement; CAMEL rating and banking ratios; banking regulations and BASEL norms; capital adequacy — SLR, CRR, CAR; asset–liability management; NPAs — problems and management efforts. Insurance: evolution, structure and development in India; insurance vs assurance; general principles and terminology; risk — meaning, risk vs uncertainty, chances of loss, peril and hazard, classification of risks; risk pooling, insurable risks vs hedging, risk-transfer methods; IRDA — duties, powers and functions; the Banking Regulation (Amendment) Act 2020.

  2. 2 · Retail Banking

    Account-opening process and documentation — KYC and AML; retail products overview (customer requirements and product development); liability and asset products; credit scoring and CIBIL; retail product marketing strategies — tie-ups for personal loans, credit cards, educational loans, auto/vehicle loans and home loans; delivery channels — branch, extension counters, universal banking, ATMs, POS, internet banking, m-banking; customer relationship management and stages of the CRM process; technology for retail banking — static information, account opening, loan origination, transaction information, disbursement and final settlement.

  3. 3 · Life Insurance

    Fundamental principles and basic features of life-insurance contracts; products — traditional vs unit-linked, individual vs group, with and without profit; policy types — term, whole life and variants, endowment and variants, annuities and pension plans; special policies for children, women and the disabled; mortality table; premium types, payment options and calculation — factors affecting premium; assignment, nomination and surrender; policy lapse, revival and paid-up value; application and acceptance procedure; claims — survival benefits, death, maturity, early and non-early claims, documents for early claims; death by unnatural causes; claims-concession clauses; presumption of death; marketing channels and bancassurance.

  4. 4 · General Insurance

    Meaning, evolution and growth; fundamentals of general insurance. Fire insurance — coverage, consequential loss, declaration policies. Marine — cargo and hull policies, institute clauses, open policies. Motor — types, third-party, comprehensive, conditions, exclusions, premium. Health & mediclaim, personal accident, liability, burglary, rural and agricultural covers, engineering and consequential-loss covers, aviation hull & liability, miscellaneous insurances. Underwriting and premium rating — proposal forms, cover notes, certificates, endorsements, premium loading. Claim settlement — procedure, TPAs, claim forms, investigation/assessment, essential documents, settlement limitation, arbitration, loss minimisation and salvage.

Core texts

IIBF — Principles & Practices of Banking, Banking Products & Services, Risk Management · Rose & Hudgins — Bank Management & Financial Services · Koch & MacDonald — Bank Management · Trieschmann, Hoyt & Sommer — Risk Management & Insurance · Insurance Institute of India — Principles of Insurance, Practice of Life Insurance, Practice of General Insurance.

Suggested cases

  • The rise and fall of a particular bank · a bank fraud case
  • Merger of associate banks with State Bank of India
  • Digital transformation in banking / insurance
  • Ayushman Bharat and the Indian medical-insurance sector

Outcomes

  • Explain how the banking and insurance sectors function and relate to the economy
  • Compare products and services across both sectors
  • Evaluate product pricing, marketing and distribution channels
  • Apply tools for financial-performance analysis and risk management

FinanceDSE-Fin03Financial Markets, Products & Services3L:1T:0P · 4 cr

An in-depth look at the structure, functioning and significance of financial markets, the variety of products traded in them, and the services built around them — the knowledge and analytical skill to navigate a complex financial environment.

Objectives

  • Understand the role and importance of financial markets in the economy
  • Analyse various financial products and their characteristics
  • Evaluate the role of financial intermediaries and services in the financial system
  • Apply the knowledge to real-world financial decision-making

Units

  1. 1 · Financial System & Markets

    Financial system — need, structure, functions and scope of financial markets; financial intermediation and intermediaries; fund-based and fee-based products. Money market: instruments — T-bills, commercial papers, certificates of deposit, call money, money-market mutual funds, commercial bills; trading mechanisms. Capital market: primary and secondary markets; methods of floatation — IPOs, book building, private placements, rights and bonus issues; investor protection and recent trends in the primary market; SEBI — objectives, role, functions and powers. Stock exchanges — NSE, BSE; index construction — weighted aggregate value, weighted average of price relatives, free-float methods; brokers and types of market orders; BOLT and NEAT trading systems; T+2 trading cycle and settlement types; pay-in/pay-out, bad delivery, short delivery, auction, demat vs physical settlement; circuit breakers and market makers.

  2. 2 · Leasing & Hire Purchase

    Leasing — definition, types and features; advantages and disadvantages; main clauses in a lease agreement; buy-vs-lease decision — tax and depreciation aspects; lease evaluation from the lessee's and lessor's perspectives; major leasing institutions in India. Hire purchase — concepts and features; evaluation with tax and depreciation implications; choosing between leasing and hire purchase; installment purchase and how it differs from hire purchase.

  3. 3 · Mutual Funds, Credit Rating & Securitization

    Mutual funds — definition and history; types; advantages and disadvantages; establishing a fund — trustee, sponsor, AMC, custodian; entry/exit load; NAV concept and calculation; SIP and SWP; the Indian MF industry and its regulation. Credit rating — definition; rating process for financial instruments; rating methodology, agencies and symbols; advantages and disadvantages.

  4. 4 · Factoring, Forfaiting & Bill Discounting

    Factoring — definition and types: with-recourse and without-recourse, domestic and international; export and import factor; single-factor, direct-export and direct-import factoring systems; fees — finance charge and service fee; pros and cons. Bill discounting — definition, nature, importance, types; bill discounting vs factoring; bill rediscounting. Forfaiting — definition, nature and importance; mechanism of a forfaiting transaction; discount and commitment fees; pros and cons; factoring vs forfaiting. Numericals on leasing, hire purchase, factoring, forfaiting, bill discounting and NAV calculations.

Core texts

M.Y. Khan — Financial Services and Indian Financial System (TMH) · Kohn — Financial Institutions and Markets · Fabozzi & Modigliani — Foundations of Financial Markets & Institutions · Machiraju — Indian Financial Systems · Pathak — Indian Financial System (Pearson).

Outcomes

  • Define key terms — lease and hire-purchase agreements, factoring, forfaiting, bills of exchange, mutual-fund types, rating agencies
  • Explain how leasing works, the stages of hire purchase and the role of credit-rating agencies
  • Evaluate factoring vs forfaiting for a company's receivables and recommend the right option
  • Analyse the mutual-fund landscape and evaluate performance metrics

MarketingDSE-MR-01Consumer Behaviour3L:1T:0P · 4 cr

A comprehensive study of consumer behavioural concepts and models to understand, evaluate and predict consumer behaviour — and translate the complexities of consumer decision-making into effective marketing strategy in the Indian context.

Units

  1. 1 · Understanding Consumer Behaviour

    Introduction to consumer behaviour; consumer behaviour and the marketing concepts; value and the consumer-behaviour framework; online consumer behaviour; organizational buying behaviour; consumerism.

  2. 2 · External Influences

    Lifestyles and psychographics on buying behaviour; social class and group influence — reference-group and family influence on buying behaviour; influence of culture on consumer behaviour.

  3. 3 · Internal Influences

    Consumer perception; consumer learning, memory and involvement; dynamics of consumer motivation; consumer personality; consumer attitudes and attitude change.

  4. 4 · Consumer Decision Making

    Concept of consumer decision; situational influences on decisions; the decision-making process — need recognition and search, alternative evaluation and choice, post-purchase behaviour; models of consumer decision making.

Core texts

Schiffman, Kanuk & Kumar — Consumer Behavior (Pearson) · Loudon & Della Bitta — Consumer Behavior: Concepts & Applications · Babin, Harris & Mohan — CB: A South Asian Perspective (Cengage) · Hawkins, Best & Coney — Consumer Behavior: Building Marketing Strategy · Solomon — Consumer Behavior: Buying, Having and Being · Evans, Jamal & Foxall — Consumer Behavior (Wiley) · Nair — Consumer Behavior & Marketing Research.

Outcomes

  • Develop a better understanding of consumer-behaviour concepts and models
  • Draw insights from the consumer purchase-decision process
  • Develop appropriate marketing strategies for different consumer segments

MarketingDSE-MR-04Marketing of Services3L:1T:0P · 4 cr

Why services need a marketing discipline of their own, the challenges they pose and how to deal with them — set against the emerging service environment in India and the world, with concepts and techniques for real services-marketing decisions.

Units

  1. 1 · Understanding Services, Service Consumers & Managing Service Quality

    Difference between goods and services marketing; challenges of services marketing; role of internal and interactive marketing; services marketing myopia; the expanded services marketing mix; levels of service expectations; factors influencing consumers' perception of service; types and determinants of service quality; the Gap Model of service quality; the SERVQUAL instrument; service-quality research.

  2. 2 · Service as Product, Delivery Process & Pricing

    Core, facilitating and support services; levels of customer participation in service creation and strategies to enhance it; service blueprinting and its benefits; customer-service standards; managing demand fluctuations in creation and delivery; service-delivery intermediaries; setting service prices — costs, perceived value and competition; revenue management in specific industries; pricing concepts — price bundling, captive pricing, two-part pricing, loss-leader pricing, result-based pricing; price-competition challenges.

  3. 3 · Service Communication, Branding & Physical Evidence

    Challenges in designing communication programmes for services; service communication problems; matching service promises with delivery; services advertising; role of promotion; the servicescape — roles it plays and its effects; environmental dimensions of the servicescape; high-contact vs low-contact services; approaches for understanding servicescape effects.

  4. 4 · Service Failures, Recovery, People & Customer Relationships

    Service failures; recovery strategies and tactics; the service guarantee and its role in achieving quality; customer feedback systems; HR strategies for customer-oriented delivery; internal marketing — delivering the promise made through external marketing; interactive marketing — managing the moments of truth; guidelines for people in service organizations; service-oriented organizational structure; customer loyalty, customer lifetime value, customer equity; a framework for building loyalty.

Core texts

Zeithaml, Gremler, Bitner & Pandit — Services Marketing (7e, TMH) · Wirtz, Lovelock & Chatterjee — Services Marketing (9e, World Scientific) · Bordoloi, Fitzsimmons & Fitzsimmons — Service Management (10e) · Hoffman & Bateson — Services Marketing: Concepts, Strategies & Cases (5e).

Outcomes

  • Understand the concept of services and intangible products
  • Discuss the relevance of the services industry and examine its characteristics
  • Analyse the role and relevance of quality in services
  • Visualize future changes in the services industry

HRDSE-HRM-08Team Building in the Organizations3L:1T:0P · 4 cr

The skill and the will to work in teams — managing yourself as an effective team member, knowing a group from a team, and collaborating across online and offline teams to weed out dysfunctional behaviour.

Objectives

  • Understand the difference between a group and a team
  • Recognise functional and dysfunctional team behaviour
  • Understand what makes an effective team leader
  • Build collaborative behaviour within teams

Units

  1. 1 · Why Groups & Teams

    Stages of team development — navigating, surviving and reorganizing through norming, storming, performing and reorganizing; why some teams pull together and some do not.

  2. 2 · Sustaining High Performance & Effective Team Members

    Goals of Stage 4 — getting the work done; managing cohesion and conflict; making team decisions; how high-performance teams function — goals, roles, norms, decisions, communication and feedback, cooperation and conflict management; avoiding blame-game politics; supporting leaders and handling dysfunctional team members; intra-group and inter-group dynamics.

  3. 3 · Developing Teams & Dealing with Virtual Teams

    Structure, organizational culture and team support; types of teams and developing yourself as a team member; evaluating team-member development; in-group/out-group dynamics; virtual teams — working through all phases of team development; conflict and collaboration in virtual teams.

  4. 4 · Effective Team Leadership & Organizational Support

    Leadership at different stages of team development; leadership and team-building style; teams and groups in organizations; building complementary team members; avoiding too much help as a leader; team autonomy and the organizational-support audit.

Core texts

Wheelan — Creating Effective Teams (Sage) · Sinek — Leaders Eat Last · Clifton — Team Management · Forsyth — Group Dynamics (6e, Cengage) · Lencioni — The Five Dysfunctions of a Team. Practical exercise: watch and review the film Lagaan.

Outcomes

  • Analyse the stages of team formation and develop team-building skills
  • Evaluate and manage conflict and collaboration in teams
  • Rearrange team formation for conflict resolution
  • Demonstrate being an effective team member in real teams

Faculty

G. Kurup

HRDSE-HRM-13Coaching & Mentoring3L:1T:0P · 4 cr

The process of coaching and mentoring and the difference between the two — the attributes of a coaching mindset, how a manager develops coachability, and how to create a coaching-friendly context with real coaches and mentees.

Objectives

  • Develop understanding of the coaching process
  • See mentoring as a developmental relationship
  • Understand coachability and the coaching mindset
  • Create real coaching and mentoring contexts

Units

  1. 1 · The Coaching Manager & Developmental Coaching

    Coaching helps employees who want to learn — so why don't more managers coach? Developmental coaching and learning; the coaching manager and emotional intelligence; the difference between coaching and mentoring; how your approach to coaching determines outcomes; a simple model of developmental coaching; the coaching mindset, the coachable learner, the coaching mirror.

  2. 2 · Coaching Mindset, Coachability & the Coaching-Friendly Context

    Can anyone learn to coach? The question of coachability and hallmarks of the coachable learner; impression management; barriers to coaching. Defining a coaching-friendly context; high-performance organizations and coaching; protecting the context and seizing opportunities to coach; working with (or without) a competency model; the coaching manager as teacher and observer; using strengths; data vs behaviour; the role of the coachee.

  3. 3 · Balanced Feedback & Collaborative Goal-Setting

    Feedback and its problems; the basics of balanced feedback; the coaching dialogue and root cause; individual and team factors; cultural diversity and the importance of getting it right; treating career and performance problems as coaching opportunities; can coaching be delegated — and will technology help?

  4. 4 · Mentoring — Film & Book Review

    Evolution, concept and wherewithal of mentoring; the mentor process and gender differences in mentoring. Book review — Tuesdays with Morrie (Mitch Albom); film review — Bhaag Milkha Bhaag, for the difference between mentoring and coaching.

Core texts

The Coaching Manager: Developing Top Talent in Business (Sage) · The Complete Handbook of Coaching (Sage) · Kimsey-House et al. — Co-Active Coaching · Bungay Stanier — The Coaching Habit · Joshi & Vohra — Talent Management, ch. 7 (Cengage).

Outcomes

  • Analyse the difference between coaching and mentoring
  • Describe and develop a coaching mindset
  • Practise developmental coaching
  • Provide feedback tied to development goals
